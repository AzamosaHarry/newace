Our Upstream Gas Supply

OML 109 (Anchor Asset)

OML 109 is a large block in the shallow waters of the western Niger Delta with more than 2TCF gas reserves. In 1991, Atlas Petroleum International was awarded the block, as Oil Prospecting Lease (OPL) 75 under indigenous sole risk concession terms. Following the discovery of Ejulebe, the licence was converted into Oil Mining Lease (OML) 109 in 1996. The operator since divided OML 109 into the OML 109 exploration area and the Ejulebe development area. Ejulebe was the only producing field on the block. Atlas began producing oil at the Ejulebe field in OML 109 in 1998. With six wells drilled and over 55 percent of the block area not covered by 3D seismic data, Atlas is seeking a farm-in partner to explore and develop prospects outside of the Ejulebe Oilfield Area. The license for OML 109 can be extended beyond its initial 20-year period to 2026 and beyond. The block lies alongside legacy oil producers such as Okan, Meji, Delta and Delta South and contains 12 prospects and 5 leads that comprise 366 mmbbl oil and liquids 2P reserves, and 2 tcf of 2P gas reserves, all located in conventional Niger Delta structural traps. Tuna South is the largest recognized oil prospect, containing 2P oil reserves of 114 mmbbl.

Odimodi Marginal Field (OML 62)

OML 62 is located in the swamp area of the central Niger Delta. OML 62 covers an area of 1,221 km² (301,715 acres) and is mostly located onshore, though the western extremity of the License extends offshore (about 8 km). Exploration in OML 62 began with the discovery of the Odimodi gas field in 1967. Successful exploration continued in OML 62 with the discovery of the Tuomo field in 1972 and the Beniboye field in 1978.

Osuopele Marginal Field

Osuopele SW Field is gas rich with a conservative estimated GIIP of about 250BCF with quite significant some upsides. Gas is meant for local market and insulated from global fluctuations in crude oil prices. Our expected peak production of about 60MMSCFd of gas over a 10 year period. Early production efforts will focus on accelerating the maturation of our oil resources as part of a phase 1 development which offers us an opportunity to commercialize the asset while we push through with our integrated gas development program. Phase 1 Oil and condensate production is expected to be in excess of 3000bopd. The time to first oil is anticipated to be no more than 1 year.

Gwatto Marginal Field

The Gwatto Field is a highly prospective asset, located over 80KM away from the most proximal gas processing facility. The field is expected to be gas-rich, with an average CGR for gas reservoirs within the corridor exceeding 30STB/SCF. As a result, significant condensate yields are expected. Additionally, existing wells were not drilled beyond 12000ft, and there are confirmed hydrocarbon accumulations from proximal fields in the swamp beyond this depth, presenting significant upside potential. Despite being approximately 10KM from the shoreline, the Gwatto Field is surrounded by proximal fields with significant gas resources, such as Benin River and Olure. Notably, there is currently no gas solution in OML 49 or OML 40, making the Gwatto Field a strategically important asset in the region.
